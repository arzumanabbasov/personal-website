Temp Project

Problem

A retail company was losing customers at an increasing rate but didn't understand why. The marketing team needed to identify which customers were likely to churn and what factors influenced their decision to leave.

Approach

I analyzed customer transaction data, support interactions, and engagement metrics to understand churn patterns. The analysis revealed that customers typically showed warning signs 2-3 months before churning.

I segmented customers based on their behavior and built predictive models for each segment. I also conducted cohort analysis to understand how retention varied by customer acquisition channel and time period.

Tools Used: R, SQL, Excel, Google Analytics

Outcome

The analysis identified that customers who hadn't made a purchase in 45 days and had contacted support in the previous month were 5x more likely to churn. Product quality issues were the primary driver of churn for high-value customers.

Based on these insights, the company implemented targeted email campaigns for at-risk customers and improved their product quality process. Customer retention improved by 15% over six months, resulting in $800K additional annual revenue.
